3. Conversion Friction — Leakage Points

⚠

Pricing page signals "budget freelancer," not premium agency

Packages starting at $149 USD are visible on the first fold of the pricing page. A US/UK buyer seeing this will immediately price-anchor low and either leave or undervalue the work. The pricing page needs a premium tier and a "Let's scope this together" CTA above the fixed packages.

⚠

No social proof above the fold

Testimonials are buried below four sections. International leads decide in seconds — a quote from Ahmed Al-Rashid (Dubai Charity) or the fintech reference should be visible within the first scroll.

⚠

Contact form has a math CAPTCHA ("What is 9 + 8?")

This is a friction point on mobile and feels unprofessional for high-ticket international buyers. Replace with reCAPTCHA v3 (invisible) or honeypot field.

⚠

No exit-intent or scroll-depth lead capture

Visitors browsing the portfolio or blog have no "soft" conversion option. There is no email capture, no lead magnet download, and no retargeting pixel visible in the source structure.

⚠

"Book a free call" CTA goes to /hire-me — not a calendar

The top-of-page urgency banner ("Currently accepting 2 new clients for Q2 2026") is excellent scarcity copy, but clicking "Book a free call" must land on a Calendly/Cal.com embed for zero-friction scheduling. A secondary form creates drop-off.

⚠

Portfolio lacks ROI/outcome language

Portfolio items show screenshots and tech stacks, but no business impact metrics. "Dubai Charity: donations increased by 40%" exists as a testimonial but is not surfaced on the portfolio card itself. Outcome-first case studies convert international leads at 3–5x higher rates.
